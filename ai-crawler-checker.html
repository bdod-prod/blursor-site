How this works

What we check, and why it matters

Most AI crawlers don't run JavaScript. A study of real crawler logs found GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot and CCBot all take the raw HTML — they never execute your scripts. Only Gemini (Google-Extended) and Applebot render. So if your content loads client-side, most AI simply doesn't see it.

This tool fetches your page the way those bots do and shows you the gap. Every check is something we can point to research for:

  • Server & robots.txt access — does your server or robots.txt turn specific AI bots away (403, disallow)? If so, nothing else matters.
  • The raw-vs-rendered gap — how much of your content exists in the HTML before JavaScript runs. This is what non-JS crawlers get.
  • Citeability basics — title, meta description, a clean H1→H2→H3 hierarchy, and structured data. Peer-reviewed GEO research ties clear structure, quotes and citations to 30–40% more visibility in AI answers.
  • llms.txt — we check for it, but we'll tell you the truth: a 300,000-domain study found it has no measurable effect on AI citations. It's hygiene, not a lever.
